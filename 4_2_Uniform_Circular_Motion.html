UNIFORM CIRCULAR MOTION

Angular velocity, centripetal force and acceleration, vertical and horizontal circles, applications

10 Lessons·KEBUNGO-R Physics Notes

Specific Objectives — By the end of this topic, the learner should be able to:

  • Define angular displacement, angular velocity and centripetal acceleration
  • Describe experiments to illustrate centripetal force
  • Apply F = mv²/r and F = mrω² to solve problems
  • Explain applications of uniform circular motion: centrifuge, satellites, speed governor

1. Angular Displacement and Angular Velocity

When a body moves in a circle, its angular displacement is the angle swept by the radius. Angles are measured in radians.

θ (radians) = arc length S / radius r
Angular velocity ω = Δθ / Δt (rads¹)
Relationship: v = rω    Period: T = 2π/ω = 2πr/v
Example 1 — Angular velocity

A turntable rotates at 45 rev/min. Find its angular velocity in rad s¹.

Solution
ω = (45 rev/min) × (2π rad/rev) / (60 s/min)
ω = 45 × 2π / 60 = 1.5π = 4.71 rad s¹
Example 2 — Speed from angular velocity

A model car moves round a circular track of radius 0.4 m at 2 rev/s. Find its period, angular velocity, and speed.

Solution
T = 1/f = 1/2 = 0.5 s
ω = 2π/T = 2π/0.5 = 4π = 12.57 rad s¹
v = rω = 0.4 × 4π = 5.03 m s¹

2. Centripetal Force and Acceleration

A body in circular motion constantly changes direction. By Newton’s second law, there must be a net force directed towards the centre — this is the centripetal force.

Centripetal acceleration: a = v²/r = rω²
Centripetal force: F = mv²/r = mrω²
where m = mass, v = linear speed, r = radius, ω = angular velocity
Example — Centripetal force

A body of mass 2 kg moves in a circle of radius 0.5 m at 4 m s¹. Find the centripetal force.

Solution
F = mv²/r = 2 × 4² / 0.5
F = 2 × 16 / 0.5 = 64 N

3. Motion in a Vertical Circle

For a mass on a string moving in a vertical circle:

At top (A): TA = mv²/r − mg    (minimum tension)
At bottom (B): TB = mv²/r + mg    (maximum tension)
Minimum speed at top: vmin = √(rg)
Key point: The string is most likely to break at the lowest point where tension is greatest. The minimum speed to maintain circular motion is at the highest point.

4. Applications of Circular Motion

Centrifuge

Used to separate particles of different densities. At high rotational speed, denser particles move outward while lighter ones stay closer to the centre. Used to separate cream from milk.

Satellites

Gravitational force provides the centripetal force for satellite orbits:

GMm/r² = mv²/r  ⇒  v = √(GM/r)
A geostationary satellite has T = 24 hours and appears stationary over one point on Earth.

Speed Governor

Uses the conical pendulum principle. As angular velocity increases, the masses fly outward, activating a valve that controls engine speed.

Review Exercise

g = 10 m s²  |  G = 6.67 × 10−11 N m² kg²

1.

(a) Define angular velocity and centripetal acceleration.

(b) A wheel rotates at 300 rev/min. Find its angular velocity in rad s¹.

2.

A stone of mass 0.5 kg is whirled in a horizontal circle of radius 0.4 m at 3 m s¹. Calculate: (a) centripetal force, (b) angular velocity, (c) period.

3.

A ball on a string moves in a vertical circle of radius 1 m at 4 m s¹. If mass = 0.2 kg, find: (a) tension at the top, (b) tension at the bottom.

4.

State the minimum speed needed at the top of a vertical circle of radius r for a string to remain taut. Derive the expression.

5.

A satellite orbits Earth at 400 km above the surface. (Earth radius = 6400 km, M = 5.97 × 1024 kg, G = 6.67 × 10−11). Find: (a) orbital speed, (b) period.
