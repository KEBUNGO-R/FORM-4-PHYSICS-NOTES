CATHODE RAYS AND CATHODE RAY OSCILLOSCOPE

Thermionic emission, cathode ray tube, CRO measurements, and applications

10 Lessons·KEBUNGO-R Physics Notes

Specific Objectives — By the end of this topic, the learner should be able to:

  • Describe the production of cathode rays and state their properties
  • Explain the structure and working of a cathode ray tube
  • Explain the working of a cathode ray oscilloscope (CRO)
  • Use a CRO to measure voltage, frequency and period
  • Solve numerical problems involving CRO readings and electron speed

1. Thermionic Emission and Cathode Rays

When a metal is heated to very high temperature, electrons gain enough energy to escape from its surface. This emission of electrons due to heat is called thermionic emission.

Cathode rays are streams of fast-moving electrons emitted by a heated cathode in an evacuated tube.

Properties of Cathode Rays

  • They travel in straight lines
  • They carry negative charge (deflected towards positive plate by electric field)
  • They are deflected by magnetic fields
  • They cause fluorescence in certain materials (e.g. zinc sulphide)
  • They have mass and kinetic energy
  • Their speed depends on the accelerating voltage: ½mv² = eV
½mv² = eV  ⇒  v = √(2eV/m)
where e = 1.6 × 10−19 C, m = 9.1 × 10−31 kg

2. The Cathode Ray Tube (CRT)

The CRT has three main sections:

  1. Electron gun — cathode emits electrons; anode accelerates them; focusing plates produce a narrow beam
  2. Deflection system — two pairs of plates (X-plates and Y-plates) deflect the beam horizontally and vertically
  3. Fluorescent screen — beam hits screen producing a bright spot

3. The Cathode Ray Oscilloscope (CRO)

The CRO is an instrument for measuring and displaying electrical signals. Key controls:

ControlFunction
Y-gain (V/cm or V/div)Sets the voltage represented by each vertical division
Time-base (s/cm or s/div)Sets the time represented by each horizontal division
TriggerSynchronises the time-base with the input signal
Voltage = Y-gain × vertical deflection (cm)
Period T = time-base × horizontal length of one complete wave (cm)
Frequency f = 1/T
Example — CRO measurements

A CRO displays a wave. Y-gain = 2 V/cm, time-base = 5 ms/cm. The trace is 3 cm high (peak-to-peak) and one complete wave spans 4 cm. Find peak voltage, peak-to-peak voltage, period, and frequency.

Solution
Peak-to-peak voltage = Y-gain × height = 2 × 3 = 6 V
Peak voltage (amplitude) = 6/2 = 3 V
Period T = time-base × wave length = 5 ms × 4 = 20 ms = 0.02 s
Frequency f = 1/T = 1/0.02 = 50 Hz

Uses of the CRO

  • Measuring voltage (AC and DC)
  • Measuring frequency and period of AC signals
  • Displaying waveforms (sinusoidal, square, triangular)
  • Comparing phase of two signals
  • Medical use: ECG (electrocardiogram) and EEG
