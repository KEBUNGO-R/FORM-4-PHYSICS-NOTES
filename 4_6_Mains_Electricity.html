1. Sources of Mains Electricity

Mains electricity is produced in large quantities by generators at power stations located far from consumers. Power is transmitted through overhead transmission lines.

2. Power Transmission

Power is transmitted at high voltage (low current) to reduce energy losses in cables.

Power loss: Ploss = I²R
Since P = VI, for same power P: higher V → lower I → lower Ploss
Kenya national grid: Generated at ~11 kV → stepped up to 132 kV or 220 kV for transmission → stepped down to 415/240 V for consumers
Example — Power transmission comparison

A power station delivers 10 MW. Compare power loss at 10 kV and 100 kV if cable resistance = 20 Ω.

Solution
At 10 kV: I = P/V = 10×106/10000 = 1000 A  ⇒  Ploss = I²R = 1000²×20 = 20 MW (200% !)
At 100 kV: I = 10×106/100000 = 100 A  ⇒  Ploss = 100²×20 = 0.2 MW (2%)
Higher voltage reduces losses by a factor of 100 (since P ∝ 1/V²)

3. Domestic Wiring

The domestic supply has three wires:

  • Live wire (brown/red) — carries current at high voltage (~240 V)
  • Neutral wire (blue/black) — completes the circuit; at earth potential (0 V)
  • Earth wire (green/yellow) — safety wire connected to the ground; prevents electric shock

Safety Devices

  • Fuse — thin wire that melts when current exceeds the rated value, breaking the circuit
  • Circuit breaker — electromagnetic switch that trips on overload; can be reset
  • Earth wire — in case of fault, current flows safely to earth instead of through the user

4. Electrical Energy Consumption and Costing

Electrical energy is measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh), also known as a unit.

Energy (kWh) = Power (kW) × Time (hours)
1 kWh = 3,600,000 J = 3.6 MJ
Cost = Energy (kWh) × Cost per unit (KSh/kWh)
Example — Electricity bill

A 2 kW electric iron is used for 3 hours per day for 30 days. If 1 unit costs KSh 25, calculate the cost.

Solution
Energy = P × t = 2 kW × (3 × 30) hours = 2 × 90 = 180 kWh
Cost = 180 × 25 = KSh 4,500

1.

State three sources of mains electricity available in Kenya. For each, briefly explain how electricity is generated.

2.

Why is electrical power transmitted at high voltage and low current? Calculate power losses at 50 kV and 500 kV for a 50 MW transmission through cables of resistance 10 Ω.

3.

A household uses: 5 bulbs (60 W each, 6 hours/day), 1 iron (1.5 kW, 2 hours/day), 1 fridge (200 W, 24 hours/day). If 1 unit costs KSh 22, calculate the monthly electricity bill (30 days).

4.

(a) State the colour code for live, neutral, and earth wires in Kenya. (b) Why is the fuse always connected on the live wire?

5.

A 2400 W electric cooker operates at 240 V. Calculate: (a) operating current, (b) suitable fuse rating, (c) resistance of the heating element.
